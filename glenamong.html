Glenamong might sometimes be overshadowed by it's larger neighbour Corranabinnia, but it boasts it's own spectacular views and challenge. For this route, park at the Letterkeen trailhead (shown below in google maps).

For the first part of the route, you will take the Bangor Trail northwest along a river from the carpark, there are plenty of signposts to guide the way. Once you reach a bend in the river and cross a metal bridge, start to make your way up the hill to your left heading west.

Once you reach the spot height at the top of the hill, head south-west towards Glenamong's East top. As you near it, the route up to Glenamong's summit should become clear. From the East top head, head West-northwest up the spur and eventually you will arrive at the summit.

From the summit there are views west across the Owenmore bog, Corryloughaphuill down to the southwest, the Glenamong valley to the southeast with Lough Feagh beyond, and the all the other Nephin Begs surrounding you. When ready to leave the summit, simply return to the carpark the way you came.
